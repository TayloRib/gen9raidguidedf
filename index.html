Generation 9 Raid Guide

Six Star Pokémon (A - C)

Dachsbun (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Play Rough:Category: Physical / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 90%
Has a 10% chance of lowering the targets Attack stat by one stage
Double-Edge:Category: Physical / Power: 120 / Accuracy: 100%
The user receives recoil damage equal to 1/3rd of the damage done to the target
Bite:Category: Physical / Power: 60 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 30% chance of causing the target to flinch
Baby-Doll Eyes:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
Lowers the targets Attack stat by one stage
Abilities:
Well-Baked Body:Category: Status
Takes no damage when hit by Fire moves instead its Defense stat is sharply boosted
Aroma Veil:Category: Status
Prevents this Pokémon from being afflicted by Taunt, Torment, Encore, Disable, Cursed Body, Heal Block, and infatuation
Tips:
Dachsbuns moveset relies on lowering the enemys Physical Attack stat, and it uses Aroma Veil to prevent its own moves from being lowered.
It has a much lower Special Defense anyway, so you can stop the main difficulty of this Raid by bringing a Special Attacker that does not have fire moves to avoid dealing with Well-Baked Body. However if you're dead set on bringing an Attacker, bring a Pokémon with the Ability Clear Body, Hyper Cutter, or White Smoke, and Moves like Haze and Mist
Although this Pokémons Attack is nothing to worry about either, bring a High Defense fighter just in case.

Ditto (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
None
Moves:
Transform:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Causes the user to become a copy of the target
Abilities:
Limber:Category: Status
Prevents the Pokémon from being afflicted by paralysis
Imposter:Category: Status
As soon as the user comes into battle, it transforms into its opponent
Tips:
The difficulty of this Raid relies on you not knowing how to take advantage of Dittos moves and abilities. The way it works is, once all the players appear on field, Ditto will transform into the Raid Hosts Pokémon. To win, all you have to do is make sure the Host brings something easy to fight like another Ditto (which will prevent the ditto from transforming or using any moves).

Dondozo (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Wave Crash:Category: Physical / Power: 120 / Accuracy: 100%
The user receives recoil damage equal to 1/3rd of the damage done to the target and raises the users action speed
Order Up:Category: Physical / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
Has no secondary effect
Heavy Slam:Category: Physical / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
The lighter the target is in comparison to the user, the greater the power (Dondozos weight in this calculation is 485 lbs ~ 220 Kg)
Yawn:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
After the next turn, this move puts the target to sleep
Curse:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
The users Speed stat will drop by one stage and its Attack and Defense stat will rise by one stage each
Rain Dance:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - Boosts the power of Water type moves used by affected Pokémon by 50%
  • - Reduces the power of Fire type moves used by affected Pokémon by 50%
Abilities:
Unaware:Category: Status
If this Pokémon uses a damaging move or is targeted by a damaging move, the other Pokémon's stat stages are ignored
Oblivious:Category: Status
Prevents the Pokémon from being effected by Infatuation, Captivate, Intimidate, or Taunt
Water Veil:Category: Status
Prevents the Pokémon from being burned
Tips:
    Dondozo is a tanky, status and move avoiding, damage boosting Champ.
  • - Oblivious makes status moves like Taunt unreliable
  • - Unaware makes your stat boosts and stat lowering moves useless
  • - Yawn makes you miss your turn without heal cheering
  • - Rain Dance boosts the damage of Wave Crash to 180 Base Attack
Pokemon with Electric Terrain, Sunny Day, and Storm Drain are a huge asset here. Not only will Sunny Day remove the Rain, but Electric Terrain will keep you and your teamates from suffering sleep effects from Yawn, and Storm Drain will pull all water moves towards itself while boosting its Special Attack. If you don't have Electric Terrain, you can use Safety Goggles as a good alternative. Other good options are Pokémon with the ability Dry Skin and Water Absorb, however note that using Sunny Day in combination with Dry Skin will cause damage to your Pokémon.
Avoid bringing Pokémon with status moves like Taunt as an Unaware Dondozo will make your support move useless.
Overall Dondozo has a pretty high Health Pool and an intimidating Physical Defense. Bring a Special Attacker with a High Physical Defense to take advantage of its low Special Defense.

Dragalge (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Dragon Pulse:Category: Special / Power: 85 / Accuracy: 100%
Has no secondary effects
Sludge Bomb:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 30% chance of poisoning the target
Water Pulse:Category: Special / Power: 60 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 20% chance of confusing the target
Toxic:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 90%
A move that leaves the target badly poisoned, its poison damage worsens every turn
Draco Meteor:Category: Special / Power: 130 / Accuracy: 90%
Lowers the users Special Attack by two stages
Acid Spray:Category: Special / Power: 40 / Accuracy: 100 %
Lowers the targets Special Defense by two stages
Abilities:
Poison Point:Category: Status
Has a 30% chance of Poisoning when another Pokémon uses a move that makes contact
Poison Touch:Category: Status
Has a 30% chance of Poisoning another Pokémon when using a move that makes contact
Adaptability:Category: Status
Increases the damage of moves that are the same type as the Pokemon when un-terastalized by 2X and when terastalized by 2.25
Tips:
Dragalge will rely primarily on its Poison moves and secondary abilities to do the most damage here between Sludge Bomb, Poison Point/Touch, Toxic, and Acid Spray.
The best defense against this Pokémon is to bring another Poison Type with High Special Defense. This will prevent poisoning, and also negate moves like Acid Spray. Consider also bringing a Steel Type pokémon, or a pokemon that knows Taunt, Misty Terrain, or Safeguard.
Dragalge has an insanely high Special Defense, so you're going to want to bring a Physical Attacker with Protective Pads or Punching Gloves.

Dragapult (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Shadow Ball:Category: Special / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 20% chance of lowering the targets Special Defense by one stage
Dragon Pulse:Category: Special / Power: 85 / Accuracy: 100%
Has no secondary effect
Thunderbolt:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 10% chance of paralyzing the target, with the exception of Electric Types
Flamethrower:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 10% chance of burning the target
Light Screen:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Reduces damage from special attacks by about 1/3rd
Reflect:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Reduces damage from physical attacks by about 1/3rd
Abilities:
Clear Body:Category: Status
Prevents other Pokémons moves or abilities from lowering its stats
Infiltrator:Category: Status
Allows this Pokémon to bypass the effects of Reflect, Light Screen, Safeguard, Aurora Veil, Substitute, and Mist
Cursed Body:Category: Status
When hit by an attack, there is a 30% chance that this ability will disable that move of the attacking Pokémon for 4 turns
Tips:
Dragapult is a challenge because it has really good damage dealing special effect coverage, can put up damage blocking barriers, can disable moves, and avoid stat changes to itself and you.
Pokemon with Psychic Fangs are a huge asset here (so are moves like Brick Break and Defog, however these pokémon are weak against Dragapult, so do not bring them). Psychic Fangs can be used by a wide variety of attackers and will help remove light screen and reflect from the field.
Bringing an Electric pokémon with Lightning Rod or Fire pokémon with Flash Fire will give you one less move to worry about, note that lightning rod protects all your teammates as well.
Please also note that cursed body cannot be mitigated by Protective Pads or Punching Gloves. You'll have to rely on some big damage in as few turns as possible, knowing that any of your attacks has the chance at being disabled, so you'll want a good range of damage dealing moves.
Dragapult has relatively low Defense and Special Defense, but only in comparison to its other stats- this is still a psuedo-legendary so its BST is 600. Your only chance at surviving here is a pokémon with High Special Defense as you cannot rely on stat altering moves or barriers for defense. The good news is that it has a low Health Pool so if you act quickly you can manage this fight.

Dragonite (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Dragon Rush:Category: Physical / Power: 100 / Accuracy: 75%
Has a 20% chance of causing the target to flinch
Extreme Speed:Category: Physical / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
No secondary effects
Dragon Dance:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Raises the users Attack and Speed stat by one stage each
Aqua Tail:Category: Physical / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 90%
Has no secondary effect
Light Screen:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Reduces damage from special attacks by about 1/3rd
Abilities:
Inner Focus:Category: Status
Prevents the Pokémon from flinching
Multiscale:Category: Status
Reduces damage taken from damage-dealing moves by half when at maximum HP
Tips:
Dragonaires biggest advantage is its psuedo-legendary BST spread with its highest stat, Attack, contributing to its pure Physical moveset. If you want to avoid extra damage from Dragon Dance, bring a Pokémon that knows Taunt.
It has even Defense and Special defense, so if you want to avoid Light Screen, you can bring a Physical Attacker without needing to resort to Psychic Fangs.
Having High Defense here is a necessity, but you also have the option to boost your Defense with stat moves like Iron Defense.

Espeon (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Tera Blast:Category: Special / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
This moves type becomes the same as their Tera Type
Psychic:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
10% chance of lowering the targets Special Defense by one stage
Psyshock:Category: Special / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
Uses the targets Defense instead of its Special Defense to calculate damage
Tickle:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
Reduces the targets Attack and Defense stats by one stage each
Calm Mind:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Raises the user's Special Attack and Special Defense stats by one stage each
Psychic Terrain:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - Boosts the power of Psychic-type moves used by affected Pokémon by 30%
  • - Affected Pokémon become immune to opponents' moves with increased priority
Abilities:
Synchronize:Category: Status
When this Pokémon is burned, paralyzed, or poisoned by another Pokémon, that Pokémon will be inflicted with the same status condition
Magic Bounce:Category: Status
Reflects status moves instead of getting hit by them
Tips:
    The main focus you should have in a fight against Espeon are its Tera Type, and its ability to use stat altering and status moves to deal huge damage.
  • - Tera Blast uses the type advantages of its Tera Type to deal special effective damage, so you need to worry about more type disadvantages than Fighting or Poison
  • - Psyshock and Tickle will make it so that focusing on a strong Special Defense means nothing
  • - Moves like Calm Mind, Tickle, and Psychic Terrain will boost a moveset that tears through your health
Pokemon with Electric Terrain, Grassy Terrain, Misty Terrain, Ice Spinner, Steel Roller and Defog are great here for removing Espeons Psychic Terrain damage boost. The real MVP will be Taunt however, since it not only disables Espeons ability to lay down Psychic Terrain, but it also prevents it from using stat changing moves like Calm Mind, and Tickle.
Overall Espeon is a pretty weak Pokémon, putting the majority of its power into Special Attack. A strong Physical Attacker here will make quick work of its low Defense and Health, but if you're playing support definitely make sure you have a High Special Defense.

Farigiraf (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Twin Beam:Category: Special / Power: 40 / Accuracy: 100%
This move hits twice per use
Hyper Voice:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Can hit Pokémon that are behind a substitute
Low Kick:Category: Physical / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
Inflicts greater damage on heavier targets up to a max of 120 damage for Pokémon weighing over about 440 lbs. ~ 200 kg
Uproar:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
The Pokémon is locked into this move for 3 turns unless interupted, effected Pokémon will wake up and be unable to sleep (with the exception of Ghost Pokémon that are not effected)
Agility:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Increases the users Speed by two stages
Abilities:
Cud Chew:Category: Status
Does not effect raid
Armor Tail:Category: Status
Prevents priority moves from being used against it
Sap Sipper:Category: Status
When hit by a grass move, its Attack increases by one stage and it takes no damage
Tips:
Theres nothing particularly special about Farigiraf aside from its High Health Pool and Special Attack.
You can counter this pokemon almost entirely by bringing a Ghost Type which isnt effected by normal moves and typically don't weigh enough for Low Kick to do much damage to. This goes both ways though, as ghost moves will not be effective against Farigirafs psychic/normal typing so be sure to diversify its move set.
Moves like Uproar and Hyper Voice can be weakened by bringing a Toxtricity with the ability Punk Rock, however it will take extra damage when hit by Twin Beams. Also be sure not to bring a Pokémon reliant on Grass Moves as it will be useless up against Sap Sipper.
Farigiraf has an equally low Defense and Special Defense so any kind of attacker is good here, you'll primarily need to make sure you have a High Special Defense.

Flareon (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Tera Blast:Category: Special / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
This moves type becomes the same as their Tera Type
Flare Blitz:Category: Physical / Power: 120 / Accuracy: 100%
The user receives recoil damage equal to about 1/3rd of the damage done, has a 10% chance to cause burning, and will cure the user of frostbite
Lava Plume:Category: Special / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 30% chance to cause burning
Will-O-Wisp:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 85 %
Inflicts a burn on the target
Curse:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
The users Speed stat will drop by one stage and its Attack and Defense stat will rise by one stage each
Sunny Day:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - The base power of Fire moves will be increased by 50%
  • - The base power of Water moves will be decreased by 50%
Abilities:
Flash Fire:Category: Status
Makes the Pokémon immune to Fire moves and will activate when hit by one, this increases the power of Fire moves by 50%
Guts:Category: Status
When this Pokémon has a status condition (non-volatile status), its Attack is increased by 50%
Tips:
Flareon will hit your team hard if you're not prepared to negate Sunny Day or Curse.
Of course you could always bring a Fire Type attacker with Flash Fire (making sure to replace your moves with non-fire type attacks to avoid activating its Flash Fire or Guts), or an attacker with the abilities Heatproof, Thick Fat, or Well-Baked Body In that case you only have to worry about damage based on its Tera Typing.
Otherwise bring a Pokémon that knows Taunt or Rain Dance to keep it from powering up, or burning your teammates.
Overall Flareon is a pretty weak Pokémon, putting the majority of its power into Attack. A strong Physical Attacker here will make quick work of its low Defense and Health, but if you're playing support definitely make sure you have a High Defense. Don't get distracted by its primary Physical damage capabilities, if you're not prepared for a Special attack Tera Blast and Lava Plume you will be thrown off guard.

Frosmoth (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Blizzard:Category: Special / Power: 110 / Accuracy: 70%
Has a 10% chance of freezing the target; in snow this move will always hit
Bug Buzz:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 20% chance of lowering the targets Special Defense for three turns
Hurricane:Category: Physical / Power: 110 / Accuracy: 70%
Has a 30% chance of confusing the target; in rain this move will always hit
Snowscape:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • All Ice-type Pokémon have their Defense raised by 50% for the duration of the snowstorm
Abilities:
Shield Dust:Category: Status
This Pokémon is unaffected by additional effects of damaging moves, but not status effects that cause status conditions
Ice Scales:Category: Status
Halves damage from special moves that hit Pokémon with this Ability
Tips:
    Frosmoth becomes a monster challenge when facing the Shield Portion of this Raid
  • - If it manages to land hits, it is doing 90 or more STAB damage each time
  • - Snowscape will increase its defense by 50% when snowing
  • - Blizzard will bypass accuracy checks during snow hitting 110 STAB damage every time
  • - Shield Dust will make status dealing moves impossible during the Raid Shield and moves with secondary status conditions less useful
Pokémon with Sunny Day is mandatory here. It will keep accuracy of moves like Blizzard at 70%, and prevent Frosmoth from benefitting from the 50% Defense bonus during snow. If you cant manage Sunny Day, bring Taunt to prevent Snowscape from happening. Another good option is accuracy lowering moves like Flash, Smokescreen, and Sand Attack that wont be negated by Shield Dust, and will help decrease the accuracy of Frosmoths powerful moveset.
The way Raid Shields work is that a Pokemon cannot be effected by status conditions as a result of status moves like Taunt during the duration of their shield. Only damaging moves with secondary effects like Nuzzle will effect through the shield. However because of Shield Dust, you won't be able to do either.
Also do not use Rain Dance, you can remove the snow effect, but you're trading Blizzard for Hurricane, which will also do 110 damage and ignore accuracy checks.
Otherwise Frosmoth has very low Health and Defense. If you bring a Special Attacker to this fight you will lose thanks to its high Special Defense and ability Ice Scales. Bring a Physical Attacker with High Special Defense to stand a chance.

  • - Moves like Calm Mind, Tickle, and Psychic Terrain will boost a moveset that tears through your health
Pokemon with Electric Terrain, Grassy Terrain, Misty Terrain, Ice Spinner, Steel Roller and Defog are great here for removing Espeons Psychic Terrain damage boost. The real MVP will be Taunt however, since it not only disables Espeons ability to lay down Psychic Terrain, but it also prevents it from using stat changing moves like Calm Mind, and Tickle.
Overall Espeon is a pretty weak Pokémon, putting the majority of its power into Special Attack. A strong Physical Attacker here will make quick work of its low Defense and Health, but if you're playing support definitely make sure you have a High Special Defense.

Pokémon with Sunny Day is mandatory here. It will keep accuracy of moves like Blizzard at 70%, and prevent Frosmoth from benefitting from the 50% Defense bonus during snow. The way Raid Shields work is that a Pokemon cannot be effected by status conditions as a result of status moves like Taunt during the duration of their shield. Only damaging moves with secondary effects like Nuzzle will effect through the shield. However because of Shield Dust, you won't be able to do either.
Also do not use Rain Dance, you can remove the snow effect, but you're trading Blizzard for Hurricane, which will also do 110 damage and ignore accuracy checks.
Otherwise Frosmoth has very low Health and Defense. If you bring a Special Attacker to this fight you will lose thanks to its high Special Defense and ability Ice Scales. Bring a Physical Attacker with High Special Defense to stand a chance.
